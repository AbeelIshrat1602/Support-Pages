What are copays and deductibles?

Copay: A fixed fee paid at the time of receiving medical services, such as doctor’s appointments or prescriptions. The amount is predetermined by your health insurance plan.
Deductible: The amount you must pay out-of-pocket for covered medical expenses before your insurance company starts sharing the costs. This amount varies depending on your health insurance plan.

How can I avoid unexpected urgent care bills?

  • Know Your Copay: Before visiting, call your insurance company to determine your copay for urgent care visits.
  • Ask for Upfront Pricing: Request the urgent care center’s pricing structure beforehand to understand potential costs.
  • Consider Telemedicine: For minor health issues, telemedicine offers a convenient and affordable option.
  • Choose Independent Urgent Care Centers: Smaller, independent centers often have lower overhead costs and more transparent pricing policies compared to large chains.

Why might big-box urgent care centers be more expensive?

Large urgent care centers often have significant leverage in negotiations due to their extensive networks and large patient volumes, enabling them to negotiate higher rates with insurance companies. These higher rates are typically passed on to consumers, resulting in higher out-of-pocket expenses.
